ROBOTICS & STEAM

What is Robotics?

And why it is Important in School as an Education?

Robotics is the computer science branch and involves electrical and mechanical engineering for the application, designing, and construction of robots. Computer programs are used in developing sensors, controller and information processing unit, so that they can follow certain instructions as and when given to them.

Robotics provides a way to introduce emerging technologies to students. With robotics students will learn Mechanics, Electronics, Electrical, Software Programming, Communications, Construction, Designing, and other related engineering applications as they build and create.

Why should it be integrated in the Curriculum?

There is an evidence that students are more interested, respond and inclined towards those subjects which are not confined to theory but involve the practical usage of knowledge and skill. And robotics is a field where you require both theoretical as well as practical knowledge.

CREATIVE THINKING

Not many fields of knowledge incorporate creativity and fun simultaneously. Studies have shown that robotics achieves both.

ENGAGEMENT

Hands-on learning activities enhance concentration and attention levels, because the more students learn physical skills, the more they want to continue being in the lesson.

PREPAREDNESS

With advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence. the present generation of students need to be more prepared for technological changes than any before.

PROGRAMMING SKILLS

As artificial intelligence becomes more prevalent in homes, schools, and offices, a little programming knowledge will help everyone understand how these bots work.
